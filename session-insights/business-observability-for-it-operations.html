3. Who Does What

PO
Product
Defines what success looks like. Who are the stakeholders? What do they expect? What happens to the business when those expectations aren’t met?
DEV
App Dev
Translates intent into instrumentation. Knows the business flows and the code. Identifies telemetry that tracks outcomes. Validates queries against real data.
PLT
Platform
Creates visibility and sustains monitoring. Builds dashboards, configures alerts, writes playbooks. Framework and embedded support that make this repeatable.
Define
Implement
Operate
PRODUCT
Define What Matters
Capture who depends on the service, what they expect, and what happens to the business when expectations aren’t met.
Define Business Signals
Name each measurement in business language — what “healthy” means and how to quantify impact when it isn’t.
Validate & Approve
Review generated dashboards and signal implementations against business reality — confirm definitions match actual operations.
Guide Operations
During incidents, reference pre-defined stakeholder expectations and impact data to classify business priority.
APP DEV
Assess Data Availability
Map each business signal to existing telemetry — Splunk indexes, databases, APM — and document gaps.
Write the Queries
Translate each signal definition into executable Splunk or SQL — good events / total events, or value against threshold.
Maintain Observability
Keep query logic, instrumentation, and signal definitions accurate as services evolve through code changes and migrations.
PLATFORM
Configure Alerting
Set warning and critical thresholds per signal, configure notification routing, write business-context alert messages.
Build Dashboards
Assemble upstream definitions into dashboards — business context panel, health signals vs. targets, quantified impact.
Create Runbooks
Document diagnostic steps, remediation procedures, and escalation paths enriched with stakeholder and impact context.
Respond & Triage
Alert arrives with business context: stakeholders affected, financial exposure, linked runbook. Triage by business impact.

4. Example: Credit Check Service

Scenario: Credit Check Service — enables loan officers to pull credit reports during the application process
Define
Implement
Operate
PRODUCT
Define What Matters
Stakeholder: Loan officers (850 active users)
Expectation: “Credit checks complete within 5 seconds”
Impact: “$2.3M daily revenue at risk if unavailable”
Define Business Signals
Health: % of credit checks completing successfully within 5s
Impact: Estimated revenue impact per hour of degradation
Validate & Approve
“success_rate signal reflects actual loan officer experience”
“Executive dashboard approved for production use”
Guide Operations
Priority: P1 during business hours — direct revenue impact
Escalation: VP if degraded > 30 min during peak
APP DEV
Assess Data Availability
Found: Splunk: credit_check_logs · Prometheus: api_latency_ms
Gap: Missing end-to-end transaction tracing
Write the Queries
Good: credit checks completed in < 5s
Total: all credit check requests
Signal: credit_check_success_rate = good / total
Maintain Observability
“Fixed timezone bug in latency calculation”
“Re-enabled trace sampling after v2.3 deploy”
PLATFORM
Configure Alerting
Warning: success_rate < 99.5% for 5 min
Critical: success_rate < 99% for 5 min → page on-call
Routing: P1 → page SRE · P2 → ServiceNow + Slack
Build Dashboards
Context: Identity, ownership, tier, stakeholders
Health: Success rate gauge + SLO burn rate
Impact: Revenue impact estimator
Create Runbooks
Diagnostic: Check credit bureau connectivity
Remediation: Failover to secondary if > 10s latency
Escalation: 15 min → App Dev · 30 min → VP
Respond & Triage
Triage: $2.3M/day at risk → classified P1
Comms: “Credit check degraded — 8s response, ~$95K/hr impact”
